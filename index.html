KorongoTaji

KorongoTaji is a community of Ugandan programmers/software developers and engineers aimed at promoting a culture of collaboration, knowledge sharing and practical skills development.

About KorongoTaji

What does KorongoTaji Mean?

The word is Swahili for grey crowned crane. This is a bird of prestige and a national emblem of Uganda, where we come from. The bird exhibits a number of great attributes that are a source of inspiration; such as its chicks being able to run as soon as they hatch. We believe this is a direct reflection of the agile/lean approach to software development.

Why KorongoTaji?

In Uganda and Africa at large, there is a growing need for local developers to develop high quality software products to meet local needs. However, not many programmers get the exposure and training required for such an endeavour. We believe that with KorongoTaji, we can tackle this big challenge.

Our main objective is to have a community that will inspire the growth of more world-class software developers/ engineers and tech products in Uganda. We hope to achieve this through collaboratively developing open source solutions to tackle challenges in the society we live in, and in so doing share knowledge and develop our research and practical skills.

Activities

What we would like to do to achieve our objective.


Open Source Projects

We believe that we learn best by doing. By working on an open source project, we learn to collaborate with others and learn from them, as well practially solving problems in our society.

Knowledge Sharing Sessions

Sharing what we have learnt with others makes us master the subject even more, at the same time inspiring others to try what may seem to be out of reach for them.

Schools Reach Out

Some of us picked interest in coding from as way back as primary school, and the people that introduced us to computers did an invaluable service to us. We too, can inspire the next generation of coders.



Coders hangouts

We would like to do lots of fun activities with other coders, to get that time out to laugh, play, dance, sing, game. We need it.

Blogging

Writing about our journeys and experiences not only helps us when reflecting on how far we have come, but will also be useful for other people to learn more about us and what we are doing.

Network

For anyone looking for a professional coder or work or business partner, KorongoTaji could be the first place to look if we build a meaningful network among ourselves.

Join us

There's a lot to be done, and we are continuously on the lookout for passionate people with similar interests. If you'd like to take part in this great initiative, please do get in touch and let us know how best you would like to contribute.
